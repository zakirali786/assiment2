In ancient manuscripts, another mean to divide sentences into paragraphs was a line break (newline) followed by an initial at the beginning of the next paragraph. An initial is an oversized capital letter, sometimes beyond the margin of the text. This style can be seen, for example, in the original Old English manuscript of Beowulf. is still used in English typography, though not commonly.[3] Modern English typography usually indicates a new paragraph by indenting the first line.
